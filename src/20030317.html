Obviously I have nowhere near enough time to be a really good blog writer.  Still, I have stuff to say, and this weblog comes with no warranty. 

Slashdot recently did a poll on spam.  I'll confess I voted for the "Grey Areas Definitely Exist" option.  The only reason why is because I don't think we all agree on what spam means.

I would agree that unsolicited commercial bulk email is always wrong.  That's what I call spam.  It definitely causes more harm than benefit.  However, I get frustrated when I see things get labeled as spam when I think they're legit. 

SourceGear has a mailing list we use to announce new products.  It's completely opt-in.  We send mail to it approximately twice a year.  And yet, we've had people throw incredible tantrums when they received that email.  If you didn't want it, then why did you sign up?

Even sillier:  I've had people gripe at me for spamming them when I sent them an personal hand-written email.  Granted, the email was unsolicited, but the world of email is severely diminished when we're not allowed to talk to someone unless we already know them.

I hate real spam as much as anyone, but anti-spam sentiment can get a little out of hand.
