Gnomedex Report
28 Jul 2003

Gnomedex was great.  The atmosphere was very different from most conferences I've attended.  I think we had 500-600 attendees.  The registration desk was staffed by Chris' Mom.  Two of the people who seemed to be a part of the conference staff were under the age of 12, and they looked like they were working just as hard and having just as much fun as everybody else. 

Des Moines was surprisingly impressive.  They say the area has around 500,000 people, which is a lot bigger than I thought it would be.  The locals were unusually friendly people.  And the skywalk system in their downtown area is just very cool.

As always, I like meeting people in person after "meeting" them in the realm of IP packets.  This time I met Randy Holloway and Brad Wilson, not to mention Chris Pirillo and his staff.  As a bonus, I happened to cross paths with an old friend I had not seen in 12 years.  Small world.

I am simply amazed at the caliber of speakers Chris was able to attract.  The big names were Tim O'Reilly, John Dvorak, Dan Gillmor and Rob Malda, not to mention speakers from Microsoft, Google, and Ziff-Davis.  The other speaker was an unknown person from an unknown company, but I seemed to be the only one who noticed that "Eric Sink" didn't really belong with the rest of those names.  Thanks to the Lockergnome staff and to the attendees for the warm welcome and all the positive followup remarks.

Chris was proclaiming this to be the most-blogged conference ever.  He may have been right.  In fact, I got "busted" because of the level of blogging at this event.  During the Q&A time after my talk, I made an off-the-cuff remark, saying that there are more VB programmers on the planet than chickens.  Randy blogged this remark, and the next day I had email from Chris Sells asking me to cite the source of my data on the world's poultry population.  Unfortunately, I was deliberately exaggerating, as a way of emphasizing just how many VB programmers there are.  Yes, there are lots of them, but it turns out there are far more chickens on earth than most people think.  In fact, they slightly outnumber the human beings.  Luckily, we're taller.
