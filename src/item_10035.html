26 Feb 2002

A surprise yesterday, albeit a pleasant one: I got an unsolicited call from a venture capitalist, inquiring about the possibilities of an investment in SourceGear. Even more surprising was the fact that the caller was from an extremely reputable firm.

The money men are cold-calling profitable software companies in the Midwest. Are companies no longer valued as a multiple of their burn rate? Does this mean the private equity markets have returned to normal? Or did they merely pass through normalcy as a brief stop on their way to somewhere else? Looks like the W3C might have done the right thing with respect to patents in "open standards".
